# Project One Hundred
## From Vision to Validation (90 Days)

**Strategic Thesis**

> *AIGINVEST's differentiation is not architectural complexity. It's Diana's ability to help real people complete real missions better than any alternative.*

Everything else is secondary.

---

## The Core Hypothesis

> **Can Diana help 100 people complete 100 meaningful missions with measurable improvement over their previous approach?**

If yes: We have a moat, product-market fit, and a roadmap for scale.

If no: We've learned which parts of the vision don't translate to reality—and we pivot based on data.

---

## Phase 1: Cohort Definition (Weeks 1-2)

### Five Mission Categories

We'll recruit 20 people in each of these categories:

#### 1. **Founders** (Starting a company)
- **Profile**: First-time or serial founders building a startup
- **Mission**: From idea to first funding
- **Success Metric**: Completed business plan + investor pitch deck + first customer
- **Diana's Role**: Question QA, market validation, pitch feedback
- **Expected Time Savings**: 60-80 hours (vs. doing it alone or with advisors)

#### 2. **Operators** (Scaling an existing business)
- **Profile**: Early-stage operators running businesses ($1M-$10M revenue)
- **Mission**: Improve unit economics by 20% or launch new product line
- **Success Metric**: Measurable metric improvement or launch complete
- **Diana's Role**: Data organization, scenario modeling, decision documentation
- **Expected Time Savings**: 40-60 hours (vs. spreadsheets + meetings)

#### 3. **Creators** (Building an asset)
- **Profile**: Writers, educators, course creators, developers building something public
- **Mission**: Complete and ship a substantial project
- **Success Metric**: Published/launched + 1000+ engaged audience
- **Diana's Role**: Outline generation, feedback synthesis, progress tracking
- **Expected Time Savings**: 50-70 hours (vs. Google Docs + Notion)

#### 4. **Investors** (Making capital decisions)
- **Profile**: Angel investors or micro-VCs evaluating deals
- **Mission**: Source, evaluate, and decide on a fund
- **Success Metric**: 10+ deals evaluated, 2+ investments made, decision framework documented
- **Diana's Role**: Deal sourcing, memo synthesis, risk modeling
- **Expected Time Savings**: 35-50 hours (vs. email threads)

#### 5. **Career Changers** (Navigating transition)
- **Profile**: People transitioning careers (TC → founder, corporate → nonprofit, etc.)
- **Mission**: Build skills, network, and land first role in new domain
- **Success Metric**: Job offer + first 90 days complete OR company launched
- **Diana's Role**: Resource curation, accountability partner, decision coaching
- **Expected Time Savings**: 30-45 hours (vs. self-directed learning)

## Phase 2: Recruitment (Week 2-3)

### Cohort Selection Criteria

Each participant must meet:

1. **Commitment**: 2-3 hours/week in the platform for 12 weeks
2. **Measurability**: Their mission has a clear success definition
3. **Feedback**: Willing to provide weekly feedback, run user interviews
4. **Diversity**: Mix of backgrounds, geographies, experience levels
5. **Reachability**: Can participate in 2-3 user testing sessions

### Recruitment Channels

- **Founder Network**: Y Combinator, Indie Hackers, startup communities
- **Creator Community**: Substack writers, online course creators
- **Twitter**: Public call for beta testers
- **LinkedIn**: Corporate operators + career changers
- **Angel/VC**: Investors + scouts

## Phase 3: Measurement Framework (Weeks 4-15)

### Primary Metrics (Quantitative)

For each participant, measure:

#### Time Savings
- **Hours saved per week**: Self-reported comparison to previous approach
- **Calculation**: (Tasks that would take X hours elsewhere) vs. (Time in Diana platform)
- **Target**: 40+ hours saved across 12-week mission

#### Mission Completion
- **Completion rate**: What % of cohort finishes their mission?
- **Quality of completion**: Did they hit their success criteria?
- **Timeline**: Faster than expected vs. on-track vs. delayed

#### Engagement
- **Weekly active usage**: Frequency of Diana interactions
- **Conversation quality**: Average length + depth of exchanges
- **Feature adoption**: Which capabilities do users actually use?

#### Satisfaction
- **NPS**: Would you recommend AIGINVEST to a founder/operator/creator?
- **Specific value**: What one thing did Diana do best?
- **Friction points**: Where did the system frustrate you?

### Secondary Metrics (Qualitative)

For each participant, collect:

#### Feature Feedback
- Which features did you use most?
- Which features confused you?
- What's missing?

#### Diana's Quality
- When did Diana's advice feel most valuable?
- When did it fall short?
- What personality/tone changes would help?

#### Workflow Integration
- How does Diana fit into your existing tools?
- Did you actually use mission context across conversations?
- How many times did you reference Diana's past insights?

#### Unexpected Value
- What surprised you about AIGINVEST?
- Did you use it in ways we didn't anticipate?
- Would you use this for other goals?

## Phase 4: Weekly Cadence (Weeks 4-15)

### Monday: Check-in
- Participant reports on mission progress
- Diana acknowledges context from previous week
- Weekly focus area defined

### Wednesday: Touchpoint
- Quick survey: "How is Diana helping this week?"
- 1 question about specific feature
- Early friction detection

### Friday: Synthesis
- Optional: 30-min user interview (1-2 per cohort per week)
- Deep dive on what worked/didn't work
- Record: Verbatim quotes about Diana's value

### Saturday: Analysis
- Product team reviews weekly data
- Identify patterns: What's working across cohorts?
- Flag: Where are people getting stuck?

That's the signal we're looking for.

## Phase 7: Learning Framework (Ongoing)

### What We're Learning

#### About Diana
- **What advice types** does she excel at? (Question QA vs. decision synthesis vs. accountability)
- **What personality** resonates? (Direct advisor vs. Socratic questioner vs. collaborator)
- **When does she fail?** (Financial modeling? Technical depth? Domain-specific advice?)

#### About Users
- **Who benefits most?** (Which cohort has highest completion rate?)
- **What's their workflow?** (Morning planning? End-of-day reflection? Weekly review?)
- **What tools do they keep using?** (Notion? Spreadsheets? Paper?)

#### About Product
- **Which features matter?** (Mission context vs. progress tracking vs. activity timeline?)
- **What's missing?** (Collaboration? Integrations? Real-time feedback?)
- **How does AIGINVEST fit?** (Primary tool? Supplementary? Reference?)

### Decisioning Framework

After 100 missions, we ask:

1. **Did Diana move the needle?** (>65% completion + >40 hrs saved)
   - **Yes** → Build for scale. Next cohort: 500 users.
   - **No** → Diagnose why. Rebuild or pivot.

2. **Which cohort saw best results?** (Founders vs. Operators vs. Creators?)
   - **Clear winner** → Specialize. Build for that cohort first.
   - **Tied** → Keep generalist approach.

3. **What's the #1 friction point?** (Diana's depth? Tool integration? Collaboration?)
   - **Addressable in 2 weeks** → Fix before next cohort.
   - **Requires rearchitecture** → Add to roadmap, communicate to cohort.

4. **Would users pay?** (And how much?)
   - **Yes** → Start piloting freemium model.
   - **No** → Identify value inflection. Keep free.

## Phase 8: Go/No-Go Decision (Week 16)

### The Choice

At the end of 100 missions, we make one of three decisions:

#### **GO: Scale to 500 (and fundraise)**
- Diana is genuinely helpful (>65% completion, >40 hrs saved)
- Product is defensible (unique Diana capability demonstrated)
- Market is real (clear cohort saw 70%+ satisfaction)
- Action: Launch public beta, open applications, fundraise Series A

#### **PIVOT: Double down on one cohort**
- Diana works for founders/operators/creators, but not investors/career changers
- Action: Specialize. Rebuild marketing + product around winner cohort
- Timeline: 4-6 weeks to pivot, then next cohort

#### **LEARN: Extend validation**
- Results are mixed. Some users thrived, others struggled.
- Need deeper understanding of what Diana should be
- Action: Run 50 more targeted missions in specific domain, iterate, try again

## Success Looks Like

**Week 8**: Early data shows 80% of founders are on track to complete business plans.

**Week 12**: Operators report average 45 hours saved. First unsolicited investor asks about partnership.

**Week 16**: 73 of 100 participants complete their mission. Average NPS: 62. Diana's "question QA" feature rated as most valuable.

**Week 17**: You know exactly what AIGINVEST should be, backed by evidence.

## Failure Looks Like

**Week 8**: Only 40% of participants are engaged. Feedback: "Diana's advice isn't specific enough."

**Week 12**: Time savings are minimal. Users say "I faster to use a spreadsheet."

**Week 16**: 35 of 100 complete missions. NPS: 28. Clear pattern: Diana doesn't understand domain-specific constraints.

**Week 17**: You have clarity on what to fix, what to build, or whether to pivot.

**Either way, you have evidence. Not guesses.**
