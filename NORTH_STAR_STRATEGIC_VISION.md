## Our Three Pillars

### 1. Intelligence (The Companion)

Diana learns everything about you:
- Your communication style
- Your goals and projects
- Your team dynamics
- Your past decisions and reasoning
- Your preferences and values

Every interaction teaches Diana to be more useful to **you specifically**.

Diana is a platform:
- Connects to everything you use (calendar, email, docs, tools)
- Extends through marketplace skills
- Embraces third-party developers
- Remains invisible to users (they just see Diana getting smarter)

### 3. Trust (The Relationship)

Diana earns trust through:
- Complete transparency
- Respecting privacy and data ownership
- Being honest about limitations
- Consistent, helpful behavior
- Supporting rather than replacing human judgment

## Five Operating Principles

### 1. User-Centric First

Every decision starts with: "What's best for the user?"

Not:
- What's easiest to build
- What maximizes engagement metrics
- What looks good in a demo
- What VCs want to hear

Yes:
- What makes Diana more helpful
- What feels natural to the user
- What builds genuine trust
- What the user will use tomorrow

### 2. Brutal Ruthlessness on Scope

We say NO to 90% of feature requests.

Every feature must pass the test:
- Does it help Diana understand users better? OR
- Does it enable Diana to do something fundamentally new?

If not, we don't build it (even if it's easy).

Alpha is narrow: chat, memory, documents, organizations. That's it.

### 3. Infrastructure First

Build the platform to support everything.

Week 1 is:
- Event bus
- Registry
- Services layer
- Real-time streaming foundation

Not:
- Pretty UI
- All the features
- Marketplace (too early)

### 4. Incremental Excellence

Every sprint improves Diana's core capability incrementally.

Not big rewrites. Not feature bloat. Focused improvements that users notice.

Example:
- Sprint 1: Diana understands your current project
- Sprint 2: Diana remembers past decisions
- Sprint 3: Diana suggests next steps (taught from sprint 2 memory)

Each builds on the last.

### 5. Transparent About Limitations

Diana is honest when she doesn't know.

Never:
- Hallucinate facts
- Pretend to understand when she doesn't
- Hide uncertainty
- Act more intelligent than she is

Yes:
- Clearly state when uncertain
- Ask clarifying questions
- Show reasoning
- Acknowledge mistakes

This builds trust more than perfect answers ever could.

## Decision Framework

**When we face a decision, we ask:**

1. **Does it align with Diana being a trusted companion?**
2. **Does it improve Diana's core capability?**
3. **Can we explain it in one sentence?**
4. **Would users notice if we shipped it?**
5. **Would we regret not shipping it?**

**All 5 yes → Do it now**
**4 yes → Schedule it**
**3 yes → Consider it**
**< 3 yes → Skip it**

## Cultural Manifesto

### What We Believe

- **AI is a companion, not a replacement** for human judgment
- **Privacy is sacred** and non-negotiable
- **Simplicity is sophisticated** - invisible systems beat visible ones
- **Quality matters more than speed** - we ship when it's ready
- **Users aren't metrics** - they're people we're serving
- **Transparency builds trust** - we show our reasoning
- **Long-term vision beats quarterly results** - we're building for 10 years
- **Team joy matters** - we celebrate wins and learn from failures

### How We Work

- **Sprints are sacred** - one goal per sprint, no context switching
- **Shipping is the goal** - talking about building isn't building
- **Quality gates are hard** - 80%+ tests, security review, performance checks
- **Async first** - synchronous meetings are for decisions, not status
- **Direct communication** - no politics, say what you think
- **Feedback loops matter** - user feedback drives every sprint
