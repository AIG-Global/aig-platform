About compliance

ESIGN (US) / UETA: no certification body exists — validity is a legal determination based on intent to sign, consent to electronic records (with required disclosures), and record retention. The real "certification" is a qualified attorney reviewing your actual process.

eIDAS (EU): this app implements the technical pattern for an Advanced Electronic Signature (unique link to signer, tamper-evident linkage to the document — see the audit trail on any document). A Qualified Electronic Signature — the tier automatically recognized EU-wide — legally requires a certificate from an accredited Qualified Trust Service Provider; that's a vendor integration, not something this code can become on its own.

GDPR: no universal certification exists. Real compliance is a legal/organizational program — lawful basis, DPAs, a DPIA (especially given identity-document handling), breach procedures — validated by qualified privacy counsel.
